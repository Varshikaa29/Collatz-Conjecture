The Collatz Conjecture Portfolio Project

Pick any number. Follow the rule. It always reaches 1 — but nobody has ever proved why.

The rule is beautifully simple. If your number is even divide it by 2. If it is odd multiply by 3 and add 1. Repeat until you reach 1. Every number ever tried eventually gets there — but mathematicians have never been able to prove this will always happen for every possible number.

The Collatz Conjecture has been checked for every number up to about 268 — roughly 295 quintillion — and it holds every single time. Yet no mathematician has ever been able to prove it must always work. It sits in a category of problems that are trivially easy to state and devastatingly hard to prove. Paul Erdős, one of the greatest mathematicians of the 20th century, said about it: "Mathematics is not yet ready for such problems."
